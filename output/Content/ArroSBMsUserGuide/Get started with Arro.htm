Get started with Arro

Arro is Income Generation software which lets you manage your income generation activities in one place. So what does it look like and how do you get started using it.

A simple browser display shows the following areas:

Header

The header identifies the school and the logged on user. The notifications bell to the left of the logged on user name alerts you to new notifications since you were last in Arro.

Navigation Bar

The Navigation Bar shows all the modules you have in ArroArro and you can select any from here. The module you select is highlighted in orange, so in the following example, the selection module is Projects.

In addition to modules, the Navigation Bar has an Admin option and a Help option.

A look at Arro‘s modules

Projects

This is where you begin. where you input information about the exciting educational projects you have in mind.

Always plan your project with thought about potential funders. What is their perspective and what will they get out of it? Will it help them achieve their objectives? This is part of building your relationship with the local community.

The Projects module will guide you through the process for writing winning narratives you can use when you apply for grants.

We know how busy things can be in schools and so you can easily share your project narrativesThe elevator pitch of your project. This is the succinct piece of text which encapsulates the reasons you have the project, what needs it meets and what impact it will have. with your colleagues and ask for their comments, advice and feedback, helping you to hone these before proceeding to make any grant applications.

Grants

Having planned your project, now you must seek potential funders for it. This is where your project narrative really begins to work for you.

Applying for funding takes time, so don’t wait until a few days before the deadline to complete your grant application. Make sure you have everything ready in advance.

There are a number of potential sources for new grants and funding opportunities that can be accessed through the Grants module Search. Through the Admin option you can see the grant deadlines. Throughout the year, expired grants are made available with new deadlines.

In addition you can find news of new grants and other funding opportunities through social mediaA number of applications that exist for online communication and sharing of information e.g. Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn..

Donations

At any time you may receive donations from benefactors. DonorsA person or organisation who gifts money or time to your school. are people who sympathise with your aims - perhaps old pupils, old staff members, parents of existing pupils or parents who want to invest in the school for their future children or grandchildren.

These will come in ad hoc and you may never have more than written communication with your donors but often you can find a way to involve them - if it is something that they want - in presenting school prizes, or naming the benefactor in school publications and so on.

Donations may come with Gift AidThis is a UK government scheme which lets registered charities reclaim a percentage of tax on donations from UK taxpayers., provided you are registered as a charity with the government and the donor pays sufficient income tax in the year.

Campaigns

Income generation may come in through different types of relationship - your stakeholderA person or organisation who has an interest in your projects. relationships. The different types of relationship or profile types are:

  • Pupil
  • Parent/Guardian
  • AlumniThe term used to describe former pupils at a school.
  • Staff
  • Person
  • Business
  • Community Organisation
  • CharityAn organisation - normally non-profit-making - which has a charitable status within the UK government's definition.
  • Organisation

The Campaigns module is where you manage your stakeholder relationships, including whether stakeholders are eligible for Gift Aid and are interesting in volunteering to help you out.

What’s Admin for?

Admin is where you set up accounts, bank accounts and groups.

Get help

Clicking on Help on the Navigation Bar takes you to the Support Website where you can find more information about using Arro.

 

Work area

Ask Pebble a question

You can ask a question directly, by clicking the Conversation icon .

Because Pebble Support staff are busy answering queries for all Pebble products, the response won’t be instant, but rest assured, if you ask a question using the conversation window, Pebble Support will respond within four hours. This is typically quicker than contacting Pebble Support by telephone or email.
