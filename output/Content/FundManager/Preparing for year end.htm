Preparing for year end

This chapter describes what you need to do before you can begin to close down the year end.

Year End Preparation Actions Check
  1. Do I know my year end date?

    2. Tip: Remember if you’re not sure when your year end is, check your start date on the Transactions section on the Fund Manager Dashboard.
  1. Have I received all bank statements up to the year end date?

    2. Note: You cannot close down the year before you have received all statements from the bank up to the year end date.
  1. Have I noted which accounts I will use in the new year?

    Tip: Keep a list of those accounts in the best way for yourself. That might be in an office document or spreadsheet.
  1. Have I recorded which accounts won’t be used again?

    Tip: Keep a list of those accounts in the best way for yourself. That might be in an office document or spreadsheet.
    • Have I cleared the balances for the accounts which won’t be used again?

Keeping track of what you are doing

For your own records, we recommend keeping a track of what you do at year end. This will help you when you come to do the year end close-down the following year.

Recommended method for doing this:

  1. Open Fund Manager > Reports > Account Summary.
  2. Select Include Out of Use.
  3. Click Export CSV.

Determine which of your accounts to close

Note: These are accounts which were used in the year you are closing but won’t be used in the new year.

  1. Open Reports > Search Account Balances.

Put the Period Dates into the date fields (you can see your period beginning date on the Dashboard in the Transactions section).

You can verify here if the closing balances are correct for the start of your new financial period. Tick the Include Out of Use box and click Search

The ‘Out of Use’ accounts appear greyed out on this report.

Now you can see all the Accounts each with their closing Balance (column on the extreme right).

You can verify here if:

the closing balances are correct for the start of your new financial period

the account will be used in the next financial period

For those accounts that are not to be used in the next period AND have a balance of zero you just need to confirm they have been marked ‘Out of Use’, (greyed out on this report).

To take an account Out of Use:

Go to Administration > Manage Accounts

Click on the box beside the account(s) that you want to take Out of Use (note you can see the account balances here)

Click in the Radio button beside ‘Out of Use’

Click Update Account(s) button (note the accounts will become greyed out)

If you want an Account balance to be something other than the closing balance on the report you will need to make an Account Transfer.

To clear a balance to zero, transfer money out of the Account, to increase the balance transfer money in. You can create a temporary holding Account for these Transfers to go into until you have determined the appropriate accounts to be used;

Go to Administration > Manage Accounts > Create New Account

Create the New Account with a Code e.g. TEMP – End of Year Temporary Account then Save

Note: Please record all these Account Transfers with a date within the financial period you are closing.

Determine which of your accounts will be used in the new year

Open Reports > Search Account Balances and make sure the box for ‘Include Out of Use’ is ticked, otherwise you may not see all of your accounts. The ‘Out of Use’ accounts appear greyed out on this report.

Put the Period Dates into the date fields, (you can see your period beginning date on the Dashboard).

You should check that all the accounts are listed with their balance that is to be retained in the new financial period and appear greyed out if they are no longer required.

Clear any closing balances of those that won’t be used in the new year

To Transfer money between Accounts;

Go to Transactions > Transfer Money Between Accounts

If you need to Transfer any Balances please record all these Account Transfers with a date within the financial period you are closing
