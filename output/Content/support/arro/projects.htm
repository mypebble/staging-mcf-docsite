Projects

The Importance of Projects

In order to achieve your vision you will need to run projects that deliver on the goals that support it. Projects almost always require financial support which you will raise through activities. You should always consider the sustainability of your projects before undertaking them. Supporters will want to see evidence of sustainability and you may need to plan to run further activities that raise finances for maintenance and repairs in future.

 

Projects are the fuel to your school or organisational objectives. So if one of your school objectives is to provide both high quality and a diverse set of sports, then one of your projects may be redeveloping your sports hall to cater for more than just football. Applying for a grant for your sports facility, for example, may be an activity you undertake to accomplish your project.

You can create community pages for your projects. These help you to promote your project and also act as a focal point for donations of time and money towards the project. Once you have created the project community page, you can promote it by sharing the URL to the page and creating campaigns to promote it. If you have purchased the Development Service, you can download automatically created flyers to send out to parents and businesses in the community about your project. You can share the project directly on Twitter, Facebook and LinkedIn, using the sharing buttons, just as if you were sharing a news article on social media.

Once donations start to come in for your project, these are listed on the project Donations tab. You can also add donations manually to the project through the Donations tab.

Create a project

  1. Open Projects.
    • If you haven’t yet created a project, click Let’s create your first Project to get started.
    • If you have created one or more projects already, click View your Projects to display the projects dashboard.

      • To add a new project, click + New Project.

  2. To create a new project, you can:
    • Base the new project on an existing template from the project library.
    • Create a custom project from scratch.

Base a new project on an existing project template

  1. Search the project library using the filters at the top to find project templates by Category, Cost or Time.
  2. Click View Template to read the template details.
  3. To use this template as the basis for your project, click Choose this Template.

    4. The new project is created and the project details are displayed.

  4. To edit the newly created project, follow the instructions in Manage a project.

Create a project, using a blank project template

  1. Click Create Custom to display the Custom Project screen.
  2. Enter the Project  Name.

    3. Tip: Choose one which is short and to the point but memorable and will grab attention.

  3. Enter a project Description.

    4. This is where you describe the project as succinctly as you can. Provide information about what you are aiming to achieve with this project and what impact it will have. Don’t worry about including everything in the description, as you can expand on your points in the further sections. Try to make a big but positive impact with the description. It should tell the story of what you are trying to achieve, emphasising the gains the school and the local community will make through the project and should encapsulate the core of the project so that you can use it as an elevator pitch when people in local businesses or other interested parties ask you about your project.

  4. The Start Date defaults to today’s date. You can change this and, if you have a completion date in mind, you can enter a Completion Date. If you don’t know the completion date or the project is open-ended, select the check box, No completion date.
  5. Enter the amount you estimate the project will cost in the Estimated Project Cost box.
  6. Click Create Project.

    7. The project is created and the Project Details are displayed.

    You can either continue to enter these now or come back and complete the details at another time. For information on how to enter more information for your project, see Manage a project.

Manage a project

Within the project details, you can keep track of the finer details of your ongoing projects.

At the top of the screen you can see the Project Name and Description. You can also see the project statistics: the Funding Target and the Amount Raised for the project.

When a project has been approved by Pebble you will see that it has been approved and the date on which it has been approved below the project name, for example .

Separate tabs on the screen allow you to add details to your project narrative, view grants attached to the project, attach other files to the project, see any donations made to the project through the project’s Community page and add donations manually to the project too.

Edit the project details

  1. Open Projects and select the project name from the Active tab.
  2. In the top section, click Edit.

    3. Note: The date the narrative was last edited is displayed below the Edit button.

  3. Amend the Project Name, Project Description, or Funding Target as required.
  4. Enter your answers to the questions.

    5. Guidance in answering the questions is given below, following a simple example of wanting to fund iPads for use in a school.

    Why Do You Need Your Project?

    		 

    Although the question is posed with a why question, your answer should really be a succinct what and why. Your answer must encompass the entire reason for the project and so adequately explain to your donors the reasons for the associated fundraising. You will need to research or have an idea of who the potential stakeholders in your project will be in order to appeal directly to them within this answer.

    For example, you want iPads available in classrooms to engage your pupils in specific interactive learning techniques available through iPad use and address the underachievement of pupils in maths or science. But who are your stakeholders? They may be pupils’ parents, even pupils themselves if they have a desire to learn a particular skill. They may be a local organisation driving for specific mathematical or scientific skills to be taught in local schools in order to harvest those later on in apprenticeships.
    You need to appeal to all of your potential donors when you write your narrative. For example, you want to invest in iPads in order to increase the employability of your pupils by teaching specific skills which they can learn through interactive iPad use; and also provide resources to the wider community.

    What Impact Will Your Project Have?

    		 

    This is determined by the what, why and who (stakeholders).

    For example, if you are buying iPads to teach specific skills, this can benefit all other pupils who may have access to use iPads in school for other lessons. It will also benefit the organisations who are sponsoring the project and it can also benefit the wider community if you offer evening classes to the general public and make use of the iPads in these classes too.

    What Supporting Evidence Do You Have?

    		 

    Source any national resources to support your project, for example, there may be research to support increase in morale and well-being when pupils are offered teaching on the most up-to-date technologies. You can also include research you have conducted in school or research conducted in other local schools.

    For example, if another school has successfully used iPads, you could use their outcomes as evidence of impact.

    How Does Your Project Link To Your School Vision and Goals?

    		 

    Link the supporting evidence you have cited to support the project back to your vision for your school.

    For example, your vision might be to be the school which offers the most innovative learning within IT within your region.

    How Do You Plan To Deliver, Monitor and Evaluate Your Project?

    		 

    You need to plan how you will deliver your project and also how you will implement it.

    For example, if you are raising money to buy iPads, plan how you will raise that money and then how you will implement new teaching using the iPads.

    How many classes a week in your own school will be affected? Will you open up the facility to offer teaching to the wider community in evening classes? Will you let other local schools use the facility within your own school?

    How will you evaluate the project progress? Will you perform surveys before and after implementation to check the engagement of your own pupils and the wider community?

    How Will You Measure and Report Success?

    		 

    You have to be able to account for the result of spending money raised for the project.

    If you were raising money for iPads to teach a specific skill, you would be able to measure the result by the number of people taking up this particular lesson, perhaps even offering the service of additional teaching to the wider community of this particular lesson and measuring the uptake of that too.

    You can measure results in any of the following ways: issuing questionnaires, performing interviews, sending out parent surveys, recording a register of uptake and interest in the facility offered by the project. In terms of reporting success, you can publish a report on the school website or distribute to the wider community through local newspapers, libraries and other interested organisations.
  5. Choose whether to save the changes you have made or undo them:
    • If you are happy with the changes you have made, click Done.
    • If you changed your mind about what you have written on this occasion and want to revert to the last saved version of the text, click Undo Changes.

View grants attached to a project

You can view grants attached to the project from the Grants tab.

  1. Open Projects and select the project name from the Active tab.
  2. Select the Grants tab. Any grants that have already been added to the project are listed.
    • Click the Grant Overview to see more details about the grant that has been attached.

Attach files to a project

You can add files to the project using the Files tab. This lets you keep all of your project information in one place.

  1. Open Projects and select the project name from the Active tab.
  2. Select the Files tab. Any files that have already been added to the project are listed here.
  3. To add files to the project, click Upload File.
    1. Click Browse to select the file that you want to upload.
    2. Enter a memorable File Name for the file you are uploading. This makes it easy to identify when you read it in the list of files attached to the project.
    3. Click Upload.
      4. Is the file taking a long time to upload?

      There may be browser issues when uploading files to projects in IE browsers. If you find an upload is taking a long time then try uploading using Chrome or Firefox to see if this fixes the issue.

View and record donations

You can view donations that have been made to the project through the project’s community page using the Donations tab and you can add the details for donations here too.

To view donations

  1. Open Projects and select the project name from the Active tab.
  2. Select the Donations tab. Any donations that have been made to the project through the project community page are listed here.

To record donations

  1. Open Projects and select the project name from the Active tab.
  2. Select the Donations tab. Any donations that have been made to the project through the project community page are listed here.
  3. Click + Record new Donation.
  4. Enter the Amount that has been donated.
  5. If the donor qualifies for Gift Aid, select Gift Aid.
  6. Select the Date.
  7. Choose a donation type from the drop-down list.
  8. Enter the donor’s name.

    9. Either:

    • Search for an existing donor name by entering the name of the donor in the Choose donor box.

    Or

    • Enter the name of a new donor in the Choose donor box.
  9. Click Create.

Share the project internally with colleagues

You can share the project with colleagues who can help you review the details and give you feedback on it.

Note: This is different from sharing the project on social media. When you are ready to do that, create the project community page and follow the instructions below on Promoting your project’s community page.

  1. Open Projects and select the project name from the Active tab.
  2. Click Share with Colleagues.
  3. Enter the Name and Email of the person you want to share the project details with and click Add Contact.
  4. Click Share Project.

    5. An email is sent to the contacts you have chosen to share the project with. The email contains a link which they can click which takes them to a page where they can add their comments.

View project comments

You can view any feedback colleagues have given you on the Narrative tab. As comments and replies accrue, a history of the feedback is built up which you can refer back to.

  • Open Projects and select the project name from the Active tab. The Narrative tab is automatically displayed.

    • Any comments that have been received are automatically listed.

Add your own comments

You can add your own comments on the project narrative and colleagues comments. These are private and not sent to colleagues you have shared the project narrative with.

  • Open Projects and select the project name from the Active tab. The Narrative tab is automatically displayed.

    • Any comments that have been received are automatically listed.

  • To add a private comment, enter the comment in the Add Comment box and click Submit.

Create and edit the community page for your project

  1. Open Projects and select the project name from the Active tab.
  2. Create the community page for the first time, edit the existing community page or view the published community page.
    • To create a community page for the first time, click + Create Community Page.

      • A community page is automatically created for you with the project details you have already entered.

    • To edit a community page that has already been created, click Edit community page.
    • To view a community page that has already been published, click View community page.
  3. To edit any of the details on the community page, click Edit.
    • You can:
      • Change the title of the community page. This defaults to the project title.
      • Change the text that describes the project.

        • You can add a link to other related web pages that relate to the project by wrapping your link in square brackets and inserting the link in normal brackets immediately afterwards. For example, [Click this link](www.example.com). This will display the text Click this link as a link in the published page and when you click on it, the web page www.example.com will open. You can open this in a new window or tab if you right-click on the link and select Open in a new window or Open in a new tab.

        Note: www.example.com is a fictitious URLAn address for an item on the Internet., used for example purposes only.

      • Change the image by clicking Change Image and selecting an image from the drop-down menu of images.

        If you want to add your own images, click +Custom and select the custom image.

        Note: You must upload the image file to the project first using the Files tab.

        Warning! Only use images that you have a licence to use and which are appropriate to your project.

      • Change the logo on the community page to your school logo.

        • Send your school logo image to us and we will insert this into your community page for you.

      • Change the colour scheme by clicking Theme and selecting a theme from the drop-down menu of themes.
      • Change the Key benefits of the project.
    • When you are finished editing, click Done Editing.
  4. To publish the community page, click Publish.

    5. The community page URL link is created.

    You can copy and share the community page link on your school website, on the school Facebook page, through the school Twitter account, through emailing your contacts and also through individuals.

Promote and share your project’s community page

You need to promote and share your community page in order to make it work for your project. We offer different ways for you to do this.

As soon as you publish the community page, you’ll see options to promote and share your page.

  • To promote your page, click + Create a campaign.
    1. Click Select on the campaign you want to send from the available campaigns displayed.

      2. Tip: There are a number of campaigns to choose from. Campaigns have different functions. For example, a campaign to tell your story is different from a campaign which asks for help or one which sends out a newsletter to stakeholders.

    2. Select the project you want to send the campaign for, from the Select Project drop-down.
    3. Select your Audience for the campaign and click Next. You can:
      • Select all contacts by selecting All from the Audience drop-down.
      • Select specific groups of contacts by selecting the groups from the Audience drop-down.
      • Select individual contacts by selecting the Include check box for the Name you want to include.
    4. Review and amend the campaign text for each campaign email.
      • Click the email you want to amend text for.
      • To make changes, click Edit.
      • Make the changes required.
      • Click Save and Preview.
      • Click X to close the Edit window.
    5. Click Next.
    6. Review a summary of your campaign and amend the details as required:
      1. Amend the Campaign Name.
      2. To amend the Recipients, click Back until you reach the Select Recipients screen. Then select the recipients you want.
      3. Select the Email frequency for the campaign.
        Every month Once a month
        Light touch Once per week
        Standard Every three days
        High touch Every day
      4. Enter the First Email send date.
    7. To save the campaign as a draft to send later, click Save Draft.
    8. To send the campaign straightaway, click Send.
  • To share your community page, you can:
    • Copy the URLAn address for an item on the Internet. link to include in an email or on your school website, for example:
      • Highlight the link that is shown.
      • Right-click and select Copy from the drop-down menu that appears.
    • Share the link to the project on Twitter.
      • Click Twitter.
        • Authorising Pebble to tweet for you?

          You are asked to authorise Pebble to post tweets on your behalf. You only need to do this once.

          • If you are already logged in to Twitter:
            • Click Authorize App.
          • If you are not already logged in to Twitter:
            • Enter your school Twitter Username or email.
            • Enter your school Twitter Password.
            • Click Sign In.

          Authorisation is recorded within your school Twitter account and you are directed back to Arro which is redisplayed.

        • Text is displayed automatically for you to share on Twitter. You can change the text if you want. Enter the text you want to tweet and click Share.
    • Share the link to the project on Facebook.
      • Click Facebook.
      • Log in to Facebook to share the link to your community page in a Facebook post.

Copy and share published project link

Once you have published your community page, you can copy and share the link at any time in the following way:

  1. Open Projects.
  2. Select the Project.
  3. Click View community page.
  4. Click Facebook or Twitter to share the project details on Facebook or Twitter, or copy the link address in the URL or address bar to include on your school website.
