Grants

The Art of Grant Writing

There is approximately £1.5 billion of grant funding available to schools and educational institutions. Because grants are a one-off source of income, they are a great way of raising initial capital for projects.

 

The Grants module is where you can search for grants you can apply to for additional funding for your school or academy. Along with all the other sources of funding available to you, grants underpin the success of your projects. So once you locate a grant that is suitable for your needs, you can save it to a project.

We want to help you create successful grant applications. We have many grants available in our database and we usually add or update them each week.

Grant applications are completed outside the project, indeed outside Arro, using the resources in external websites which provide the grants or following their guidelines. However, when you apply for a grant, you should keep in mind which project it will support and understand how the grant will support the project aims. The three most important things to keep in mind when applying are:

  • To align the vision and goals of your application for your project with those of the grant donor.
  • To tell a story, identifying a need and showing the positive impact that will result from meeting that need with the grant.
  • To demonstrate planning to achieve the desired result and provide an explanation of how the result will be measured.

Where are grants listed?

All available grants are listed in the Grants module.

  • Open Activities > Grants.

You can view all grants in the database by clicking View all or view only those that you can apply for - based on your location and type of school - by clicking Show me grants I’m eligible for.

Searching for a grant

You can search for grants for different types of school and by location, by clicking and selecting the type and location from the links at the top of the Grants dashboard.

  • Click the first drop-down to select the Type of School.
  • Click the second drop-down to select the School Region.

You can also drill down to find grants by Project Type (e.g. to find grants for languages or for physical education), by Grant Type, by Complexity (the amount of detail required and the amount of time it will take to complete your application) or by Status (e.g. new, updated or expiring grants).

You can use the scroll bar on the drop-down list to scroll down the Filter By selections or you can use the scroll wheel to do this. The scroll wheel is located between the left and right mouse buttons on your mouse.

Tip: To use the scroll wheel, make sure the cursor is located on the drop-down you want to scroll before using the scroll wheel.

What types of grant are there?

Type of Grant Icon Description

Cash

Cash grants provide you with support in the form of money that is available in particular areas of interest e.g. IMA Education Grants Scheme or Sir John Cass’s Foundation.

Freebie

Freebie grants include:

  • Government funded or various organisational schemes for promoting subjects e.g. Further Maths.
  • Discounts that enable you to buy goods and services cheaper, or obtain cash back for the school when the discount is applied, for example, Football Foundation: Respect.
  • Schemes which you can take part in to assist you in fundraising e.g. Images.
  • Free resources you can use to supplement teaching in school e.g. English Heritage Free School Visits.

Awards

A competition is a way of providing resources to your school for the best entrant in a particular endeavour (e.g. Tycoons in Schools competition).

Is a grant suitable for you?

Have a look at the grant details.

When you view a grant, read the Who is Eligible? section to see if you qualify, are able to apply for it and whether getting the grant will support your project aims and vision.

If so, then find out what you can about projects the funding organisation has previously funded as this will assist you when you make your own application.

View grant details

  • Click the hyperlinked title in the Grant Overview.

    • A brief outline of the award is displayed and you can see at a glance what you will get, how difficult it is to apply and whether there is a deadline or not.

    • To find out if you are eligible for the grant, read the Who is Eligible? section. You will also find out what you need to do next to apply for the grant. This often includes applying directly through another website. In this case, a link is supplied to the website where you will find detailed instructions on how to apply for the grant.
    • To save the grant, click Add to Favourites.
    • To add the grant to your project, click Attach to Project.
    • To close the grant description, click X.

Attach grants to projects

You can attach grants to projects. Once you do this you will see the grants listed in the Project Details > Grants tab.

  • Click the hyperlinked title in the Grant Overview.
  • Click Attach to Project alongside the Project Name displayed in the Grant details window.

Save grants as favourites

You can save grants to access quickly by marking them as favourites. You can:

  • Click the star to the left of the grant name in the list of grants . The star will turn yellow to show that you have selected it.
  • Click Add to Favourites within the grant description. The star to the left of the grant name in the list of grants will turn yellow to show that you have selected it.

View favourite grants
