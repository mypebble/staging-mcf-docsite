Manage Payments

You can manage payments using Arro‘s integrated payment portal which uses Stripe payments gateway software to manage the payments.

Prerequisites

Pebble sets the payment module up for you when you sign up with us to use it. You tell us:

  • The number of accounts you need.

    • You may have different accounts for donations and for services and lettings, for example.

  • The payment schedule you want to work with.

    • Once you start processing payments, your first payout is made between seven and ten days after your first successful payment is received. Thereafter, the payment schedule determines when you get payouts. The default is weekly on a 7 day rolling schedule. This means that payouts are made daily and contain payments made seven days prior to the payout date.

Note: Because the module makes use of Stripe to handle the payments, make sure to read Stripe’s Services Agreement.

Payment charges

There is a 6% fee for donations made through the Joinos Community which covers the cost of processing the transaction through Stripe and all the work we do to maintain your Community Page. When your donors make a payment, they can choose to cover the fee as part of their transaction so the school, in effect, pays nothing. There is also a fee for Services and Lettings bookings.

Note: When you make refunds, everything that has been paid is refunded, including the transaction charge.

Accessing the payments module

  • Go to https://payments.joinos.com/merchant/ and click Login with Arro.

Your Account List is displayed and will show a list of the accounts we have set up for you according to your requirements.

From the Account List you can view transactions, invoices and subscriptions that have been set up.

Invoices

To raise an invoice

  1. From the Account List, click Invoices for the account you want to raise an invoice for.
  2. Click Create Invoice.

    3. The New Invoice screen appears.

  3. Enter the customer name (person or organisation) in the Invoice To box.
  4. Enter the Invoice Reference or Invoice Number.

    5. Note: These are for your reference and so you should follow your usual practices for referencing or numbering invoices.

  5. Enter the date when the invoice is due by in the Due by box.
  6. Enter the invoice Line Items.
    • To add a new line item, click + Add New.
    • To remove a line item, click Remove alongside the line item to remove.
    • Enter the following information for each line item:

      • The Tax amount is calculated and displayed automatically when you enter the Tax Percentage.

  7. If the invoice is recurring, select Recurring Invoice.
    • Enter the details for when the invoice should recur e.g. every 1 month on the 25 means that every month the invoice will be issued on the 25th of the month.
  8. Click Save.

    9. The invoice is raised and a link inserted at the top of the invoice which you can send to the customer to enable them to pay the invoice.

To request payment of an invoice

Create an email, using your email provider and send the invoice link to the customer to pay.

When the customer receives the email from you they:

  1. Click the link provided which then displays the invoice for payment.
  2. Click Pay online instantly.
  3. Enter the Email address, Card number, MM/YY expiry date, card CVC code.
  4. If the customer wants the payment gateway to retain this information, they select Remember me.
  5. If the invoice is a recurring invoice, click Subscribe for [amount], otherwise click Pay [amount].

    6. Note: When a customer pays a subscription they receive a receipt email outlining what they have paid for and containing a link which lets them manage their own subscription.

To check if an invoice has been paid

  • From the Account List, click Invoices for the account you want to view invoice details for.
    • If an invoice has been paid there is a green tick to the left of the invoice in the Invoice Summary list.

To view invoice details

  1. From the Account List, click Invoices for the account you want to view invoice details for.
  2. In the Invoice List, locate the invoice you want to view details for and click Open.
  3. Click the link to the invoice.

    4. You can see the invoice details including the link to the invoice that was sent out to the customer.

Transactions

To view transaction details

  1. From the Account List, click Transactions for the account you want to view transactions for.

    2. The Transaction List shows the following information, by default: transaction Date, Description, Gross amount, transaction Fees, Net amount, whether the transaction has been Refunded, transaction Customer, Source (e.g. Joinos Community Page Donation or Joinos Payment Invoice )and Actions you can perform for the transaction.

    Note: If you feel that different information would be more helpful for you, please contact Pebble Support and we will be able to adjust the transaction view for you.

  2. Find the transaction you want to view the details for and click Details.

    3. In addition to the summary details you could see in the Transaction List for the transaction, you can also see the Service and unique Reference for the transaction.

    Note: If you ever need to contact Pebble Support about a transaction, for example, a system going down during the refund process, quote the transaction unique Reference in correspondence.

To refund transactions

  1. From the Account List, click Transactions for the account you want to refund from.
  2. Find the transaction in the Transaction List.
  3. Click Details.

    4. Warning! Double-check the details to ensure you are refunding the correct payment as you cannot reverse a refund once made.

  4. Click Refund Transaction.

    5. Warning! Again check you are sure you want to refund the transaction.

    • Click Refund Transaction.

      • The transaction is refunded in full (including transaction fees).

      The transaction is marked with the date of the refund in the Refundedcolumn of the Transaction List.

      Note: It may take a couple of days for the money to reach the customer’s bank account.

To export transactions to CSV file format

  1. From the Account List, click Transactions for the account.
  2. Click Export to CSV.

    3. The transactions are automatically downloaded to a file named transactions.csv.

Subscriptions

Subscriptions can be raised by creating recurring invoices or payment requests.

To view subscription details

  1. From the Account List, click Subscriptions for the account you want to view subscriptions for.

    2. The Subscription List shows the following information: Created, Started, Cancelled, Subscription Description, Amount, Customer, Source and Actions you can perform for the transaction.

  2. From the Subscription List, click Details.

To cancel a subscription

You can cancel a subscription for your customer or the customer can cancel it using the link in the receipt that they receive when they first pay the subscription invoice or payment request.

How do you cancel a subscription for the customer?

  1. From the Account List, click Subscriptions for the account you want to cancel a subscription for.
  2. Locate the subscription and click Details.

    3. Warning! Double-check the details to ensure you are cancelling the correct subscription as you cannot reverse a cancellation once made.

  3. Click Cancel Subscription.

    4. Warning! Again check you are sure you want to refund the transaction.

    • Click Cancel Subscription.

How does the customer cancel a subscription?

  1. In the email receipt, click the link manage your subscription here.
  2. In the Manage your subscription window, click Cancel subscription.

Payment Requests

To raise a payment request

  1. From the Account List, click Payment Requests for the account you want to raise a payment request for.
  2. Click Add New.

    3. The New Payment Request screen appears.

  3. Enter the Title for the payment request.
  4. Enter the Description for the payment request.
  5. Enter the Amount you are requesting.
  6. If the payment request is for a subscription, select Recurring Payment.
    • Enter the details for when the payment should recur e.g. every 1 month on the 25 means that every month the payment will be on the 25th of the month.
  7. Click Save.

    8. The payment request is raised and a link inserted at the top of the request which you can send to the customer to enable them to make the payment.

To request payment

Create an email, using your email provider and send the payment request link to the customer to pay.

When the customer receives the email from you they:

  1. Click the link provided which then displays the invoice for payment.
  2. Click Pay if a one-off payment, or click Subscribe if a recurring payment or subscription.
  3. Enter the Email address, Card number, MM/YY expiry date, card CVC code.
  4. If the customer wants the payment gateway to retain this information, they select Remember me.
  5. If the invoice is a recurring invoice, click Subscribe for [amount], otherwise click Pay [amount].

    6. Note: When a customer pays a subscription they receive a receipt email outlining what they have paid for and containing a link which lets them manage their own subscription.

To check if a payment request has been paid

  • From the Account List, click Transactions for the account you want to view details for.
  • The one-off or recurring payment transaction will show up in this list if it has been paid.

To check if a recurring payment request has been subscribed

  1. From the Account List, click Subscriptions for the account you want to view details for.
  2. In the Subscription List, locate the transaction you want to view details for and click Details
