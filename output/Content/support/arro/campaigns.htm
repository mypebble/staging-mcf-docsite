Campaigns

The Importance of Campaigns

Campaigns are one of the most important ways of communicating with your contacts, sharing information and asking for help. You can run email or social media campaigns, depending on the campaign you are running and the audience you want to reach.

 

The Campaigns module is where you create and view any marketing campaigns you have sent to your contacts by email or shared on Twitter. You can work on campaigns you want to send out and save these as drafts until you are ready to go with them.

Campaigns help you to maximise your engagement with your contacts. You can run different campaigns, for example, to help you recruit volunteers or share information and generate excitement about your project. You can send email campaigns or run a social media campaign using Twitter.

If you choose to send an email campaign, the number of emails sent depends on the campaign you select. For example, the Monthly Newsletter campaign sends one email while the Supporters’ Evening campaign sends four. You choose the contacts to send the campaigns to and when more than one email is sent in a campaign you choose the timeline too.

After you have run an email campaign, the campaign is added to the Email tab. Campaigns listed there will have a status of Scheduled or Complete.You can preview which emails have been sent in the campaign by clicking the Expand icon alongside the campaign Name.

The Email drafts tab displays the email campaigns you have previously saved as drafts. You can work on them by clicking the draft name. You can preview which emails are scheduled to send in the campaign by clicking the Expand icon alongside the campaign Name.

If you choose to run a social campaign using Twitter, you can view the tweets that have been tweeted or are scheduled to tweet on the Social tab. Twitter campaigns contain tweets that will automatically be tweeted at 7 am, noon and 8 pm on consecutive days.

You can create more than one Twitter campaign and if you don’t want them to go out at the same time, just ensure that you schedule the start date of subsequent campaigns for after the previous campaign completion date.

Create a campaign

    1. Open Campaigns.
    2. Select Email or Social.
    3. If you select Email follow these steps.
      1. Click + New Campaign.
      2. Click Select on the campaign you want to send from the available campaigns displayed.

        3. Tip: There are a number of campaigns to choose from. Campaigns have different functions. For example, a campaign to tell your story is different from a campaign which asks for help or one which sends out a newsletter to stakeholders.

      3. Select the project from the list of available projects and click Next.

        4. Tip: If you can’t see the project and you expect to, go to Projects and ensure you have marked the project as active and have published the community page for the project.

      4. Select your Audience for the campaign and click Next. You can:
        • Select all contacts by selecting All Contacts.
        • Select specific groups of contacts by selecting the groups.
      5. Preview the campaign by clicking on the campaign box.
        • To make changes, click Edit.
        • Make the changes required.
        • Click Save and Preview.
        • Click X to close the preview window.
      6. Click Next.
      7. Review a summary of your campaign and amend the details as required:
        1. Amend the Campaign Name.
        2. To amend the Recipients, click Back until you reach the Select Recipients screen. Then select the recipients you want.
        3. Select the Email frequency for the campaign.
          Every month Once a month
          Light touch Once per week
          Standard Every three days
          High touch Every day
        4. Enter the First Email send date.
      8. To save the campaign as a draft to send later, click Save Draft.
      9. To send the campaign straightaway, click Send.
      10. Click Back to Campaign List.
      4. If you select Social follow these steps.

      If this is the first time you have used Twitter from within Arro, you must log in and authorise Pebble to tweet for you.

      Authorising Pebble to tweet for you?

      You are asked to authorise Pebble to post tweets on your behalf. You only need to do this once.

      • If you are already logged in to Twitter:
        • Click Authorize App.
      • If you are not already logged in to Twitter:
        • Enter your school Twitter Username or email.
        • Enter your school Twitter Password.
        • Click Sign In.

      Authorisation is recorded within your school Twitter account and you are directed back to Arro which is redisplayed.

      You are presented with some pre-populated tweets for the campaign. Tweets are made three times a day at 7 am, noon and 8 pm GMT on successive days.

      1. To change tweet text, click in a Tweet box and amend the text.
      2. To add a tweet, click New Tweet to add a blank box at the foot of the list of tweets and then enter the text you want to tweet.
      3. To remove a tweet from the list, click X alongside the tweet.
      4. When you have set up the tweets for the campaign, click Schedule.
      5. Enter the First Tweet Date.

        6. This defaults to today’s date but you can set it to be what you want so you can create a number of Twitter campaigns and schedule them so that they don’t overlap.

      6. Click Start Tweeting.
      7. Click Back to Dashboard.
      Note:

      The schedule of tweeting depends on the Start Date and the time of day you click Start Tweeting. For example, if you schedule three tweets tomorrow these will be tweeted at 7 am, noon and 8 pm on the same day. If you select today’s date as the Start Date but click Start Tweeting at 11 am, the tweets will go out at noon, 8 pm and 7 am the next day.

      If you have scheduled a Twitter campaign and need to amend or cancel the tweets, contact Pebble for assistance.

Completing a draft email campaign

  1. Open Campaigns > Email drafts.
  2. Click the draft name you want to complete.

    3. The Preview your campaign screen, that is displayed but you can click Back to change the audience if required. Otherwise, click on the Email to preview and amend the text, if required.

  3. To come back and complete this campaign later, click Save Draft.

    4. To send the campaign, click Send.

Collecting Gift Aid delcarations

Run this campaign to help you contact all eligible stakeholders and collect Gift Aid declaration forms from them. If you collect this, in addition to the 25% uplift on each individual donation, you can claim an additional 2% on all Gift Aid Eligible Transactions and Donations.

  1. Click Run Campaign.
  2. Click Download Template to download the Gift Aid template to send to stakeholders.
  3. Click Download Data to download a list of stakeholders who have not yet provided Gift Aid details.
  4. Create and send a letter to send to each stakeholder in the list produced in step 2 with a copy of the Gift Aid template produced in step 1.
  5. Then select Send Letters.
  6. Click Complete Campaign.
