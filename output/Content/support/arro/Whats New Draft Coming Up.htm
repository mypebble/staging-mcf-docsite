What's new xth November 2017?

Joinos Community Site

Announcing the arrival of your Joinos Community Site, the place where you can manage all of your project community pages in one place. For more information, see the Joinos Community Site Home Page.

Project News

You can write and post articles about your projects now using the News module in Arro.

For more information, see News.

Previous releases

Released 17th November 2017

Gift Aid

You can now collect Gift Aid declarations from your existing contacts in Arro. From the contact details, click Add Gift Aid Declaration.

From the Add Gift Aid Declaration form, you can send an email to the contact asking them to fill in their Gift Aid declaration online. You can also upload a declaration form and store it online for the contact. And if you need to you can download a Gift Aid Declaration template.

For more information, see Contacts.

Released 13th November 2017

Project Joinos Page

Your existing project community page is now called a project Joinos page. It looks slightly different but is easier for you to manage and more engaging for your community.

For information on the project Joinos page, see Projects, Projects Pages and Help on Supporting Projects

Manage your Organisation’s Details

You can now upload your own text and logo for the school, using the Profile option on the drop-down menu below your logon name.

For information on managing your details, see About our School Page.

Pebble Learn

Pebble Learn is a flexible and convenient way to access your Income Generation training. When you purchase a subscription to Arro, we walk you through all you need to know about setting up and using Arro to manage your projects and income generation. We also enroll you in our online training platform, Pebble Learn. Once your email has been verified by us, you will be able to access your online training courses from the Pebble Learn option on Arro's Navigation bar.

For more information, see Using Pebble Learn.

Released 30th October 2017

Grants

You can now click Apply for Grant on the Grant Details screen, once you have attached the grant to a project. This transfers you automatically to the grant website for you to make the application.

There are new grant statuses which help you keep track of your grant applications and we encourage you to record the value of any grant awards within Arro. This makes it easier to track the amount of income you are generating for your projects through external funding bodies.

For more information, see Grants.

Payment Gateway

You can now request one-off or recurring payments from your contacts, by creating the request within Joinos Pay and then creating an email campaign to send to your contacts.

For more information on setting up payment requests, see Manage Payments.

Released 18th October 2017

Getting started with Arro

There’s a new Guide on the Support Website which outlines what this includes and what you can expect from us in terms of onboarding.

For more information, see the Arro Onboarding Guide

Released 5th October 2017

Projects

We understand that you get occasional ad hoc donations to projects so we’ve added the ability to add donations to the project manually, using + Record new Donation on the Projects Donations tab. This makes it easy for you to add these donations directly to the project.

For more information, see Projects.

Sponsors and Volunteers

Sometimes sponsors or volunteers drop out and so we’ve added the ability to remove sponsors and volunteers but retain the history of your connection with them. You can now archive sponsors and volunteers and if this is temporary you can restore them at a later time.

For more information, see Sponsorship and Volunteers.

Scrolling in drop-downs - how to do it

We understand that some of you have been asking how to scroll down the options in drop-downs.

This isn’t new but we want you to understand how you can do this more easily.

You can scroll in drop-downs either using the scroll bar in the drop-down or using the mouse scroll wheel when the mouse cursor is placed on the drop-down list.

 

For more information on using Arro, see Getting to know Arro .

Released 27th September 2017

Projects

We’ve made some changes to make it easier for you to work on your project narratives.

  • You can now add your own private comments.
  • You can now work on a project narrative and Undo Changes if you want to revert to the previous version.
  • You can see the last edited date and the name of the person who edited the project narrative under the Edit button.

For more information, see Projects.

Campaigns

We’ve made it easier for you to manage your email and social campaigns.

  • You can now preview which emails have been sent in email campaigns by clicking the Expand icon alongside the campaign Name on the Email tab.
  • You can now add unlimited tweets to your Twitter campaigns. Tweets go out three times a day: at 7 am, noon and 8 pm and you can now see feedback on your Twitter likes on the project dashboard.

For more information, see Campaigns.

Managing payments

We have improved how you can receive and manage online payments.

Released 8th September 2017

Brand new school year, brand new Arro

We’ve listened to you and redesigned Arro to help you perform your income generation activities more easily and intuitively.

If you have never used Arro before this won’t mean anything to you, but if you have you’ll notice we have moved some things around to help you access what you need more easily.

  • There’s a new Arro dashboard.

    • The new Arro dashboard gives you an overview of your income generation. You can see at a glance:

    • How much your community (stakeholders, sponsors and volunteers) has donated.
    • How successful your campaigns are and get some tips on how to get a better openThe number of times a marketing campaign email has been opened. and click rateIndicates the number of times the link to your community page has been clicked. This can be from a hyperlink in an email during a marketing campaign, for example..
    • What actions will help you progress your income generation plans.

    For more information about the dashboard, see Getting to know Arro .

  • The navigation bar has been simplified to highlight the most important things in income generation - Projects, Activities, Campaigns and Supporters.
    • Projects is where you define your projects and what you are trying to achieve, advertise this through each project’s Community Page and collect donations for the project through the Community Page, displaying these on the Project Donations tab.
    • Activities is where you access those activities which support your income generation - Services & Lettings and Grants.
    • Campaigns is where you build email and social media campaigns to reach out for support for your projects.
    • Supporters is where you build and keep a list of those people and organisations who support what you are doing. As well as managing your Contacts list, this is where you manage Sponsorship and Volunteers.

In addition, Arro screens are bolder in colour, images are enhanced and field names simplified, all making it easier for you to use.

Quick access to help finding your way around

  • To log on to the redesigned Arro, see Accessing Arro
  • For information on using Arro, read Getting to know Arro
  • If you need help, Getting help explains how you can.
