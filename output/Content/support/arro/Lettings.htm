Lettings

Tip: You can view screens on this page at full size by clicking on the thumbnail image.

Earning income through your premises
You will find that you can hire out and use your school premises or property when it’s not being used during the school day and this will have two effects - it brings in more revenue to the school and it increases the community awareness about the school and it’s values and what it can offer the community.
In addition you can also trade your lettings with other schools, filling in the gaps for each other and generating revenue for all of you.

The Lettings module is where you manage the portfolio of your school’s premises for hire.

In the Lettings module, you can:

  • Record and audit the lettings you offer.
  • Market your portfolio.

You can build your portfolio by adding items from scratch or by adding lettings from a number of templates (e.g. meeting room, playing field, gym) and tailoring these to match your own offering. You can add images of the facilities and terms and conditions documents, so all of the information about the items on offer is held in the same place.

How do I build a lettings portfolio?

When you first begin to use this module the lettings dashboard will be empty and you are prompted to build your portfolio. You can add items from scratch or select from a library of templates and amend the details to match what your school offers.

First time use
  1. Click Lettings.

    2. The Get Started screen is displayed.

  2. To watch an introductory video about services and lettings, click the Play button .
  3. To begin creating your portfolio, click Get Started. The Select Your Lettings page is displayed. This shows a library of templates, for generic facilities.

  4. Click Select on the templates which match what you have and would like to let.
  5. Click Continue.

    6. Details for the first template you selected are displayed for you to amend and complete. The others you selected are added to the lettings dashboard as Incomplete so you can edit these in turn after the first.

    Note: If you want to change the templates you chose, you can click Back and can then click Back to selection to return to the selection screen and choose different templates.

  6. Edit the details.

    7. Note: To make a letting ready to publish, you must add a contact, a contact phone number and at least one image.

    1. Amend the details so these describe your facility.
    2. Select a user from the Contact drop-down list and enter a Contact Phone Number.
    3. Add images for your letting.
      1. Click + Add Image to add an image.
      2. Click Choose a file.
      3. Select the image to upload and click Open.
      4. Click Upload.

        5. Tip: If you upload an image you didn’t mean to, select the image. click Delete and confirm the deletion when prompted.

      5. If you upload more than one image, select which image is the primary image.

        6. Note: If you upload only one image this is the primary image by default.

  7. To save the details:
    • To save the details and publish later, click Save and publish later.
    • To publish now, click Publish now.

After first time use, the dashboard displays the lettingsyou have chosen previously and their status.

You can edit an item, by clicking Edit. You can see how it might look before it has been published by clicking Preview. You can visit the published page by clicking Visit Page.

You can create lettings either from a template or from scratch, by clicking + New Letting.

Once you have published lettings, the link to the published page is displayed at the top of the screen, along with Facebook and Twitter buttons for you to share details on social media. You can select the lettings you want to feature most prominently on your published Lettings page. If you don’t select any, we will select these automatically for you to ensure your page looks as good as it can.

Create a letting, using a blank template

  1. Open Lettings.
  2. Click +New Letting

    3. .

  3. Click Select on Custom Letting to display the Edit Your Letting screen.
  4. Enter the Letting Name.

    5. Tip: Choose a title which is short and to the point but easily identifies the facility e.g. Gym, Meeting Room.

  5. Enter your Summary. This is a short description e.g. Fitness Gym with Weights, We have multiple meeting rooms, ideal for between 2 and 50 attendees.
  6. Select a Contact from the drop-down list of contacts.
  7. Enter the Contact Phone Number.
  8. Enter the Description. This is an expansion on the summary.
  9. Enter the Key Features. These are the key selling points e.g. Bench Press, Disabled Access, Air Conditioning, Wifi, Tea and Coffee, etc.
  10. Enter the Details for the hire. This is an expansion on the Description and can include full details of the facilities offered with the hire and whether there are conditions for the letting or activities which are suitable.
  11. Enter the Availability, for example, the dates and times the facility is available.
  12. Enter the Prices, for example, the rates of hiring, whether a deposit is required, what cancellation costs there are.
  13. If you have testimonials to include, enter the text in the Testimonial box. You should also enter the name of who has given the testimonial in the Endorser box.
  14. If you have someone who has used the facility and is willing to give a recommendation of it, you can enter their name in the Endorser box.
  15. Add images for your letting.
    1. Click + Add Image to add an image.
    2. Click Choose a file.
    3. Select the image to upload and click Open.
    4. Click Upload.

      5. Tip: If you upload an image you didn’t mean to, select the image. click Delete and confirm the deletion when prompted.

    5. If you upload more than one image, select which image is the primary image.

      6. Note: If you upload only one image this is the primary image by default.

  16. To save the details:
    • To save the details and publish later, click Save and publish later.
    • To publish now, click Publish now.

Archiving lettings

  1. Open Lettings.
  2. Click Edit on an Incomplete, Ready to publish or Published letting.
  3. Click Archive.

Restoring archived lettings

  1. Open Lettings.
  2. Click Restore on an Archived letting.

    3. The letting restores with its previous status. For example, if it was published before being archived, it is restored as published. How do I publish and promote my lettings portfolio?

You can choose the lettings you want to feature prominently on your published lettings for hire page, although you do not need to wait to finish creating your portfolio before you publish the lettings which you are ready to market. You can work on draft lettings and only publish the ones with complete information.

Once you have published a letting, you can begin to promote it, by linking to your published lettings page from the school website or by sharing its URL through social media.

    The details are published on your hire.joinos.com page. And Published is displayed at the top of the letting details.

Manage letting enquiries

When you publish and share details of your lettings people can enquire about booking them, either through phoning the contact phone number for the facility or filling in the online booking form.

You can manage enquiries that come in via the online booking form on the Enquiries tab in the Lettings module.

An enquiry has one of the following statuses:

Pending

The enquiry has come in via the online booking form on hire.joinos.com . The terms of the booking need confirmation - this might or might not require enquiry will retain this status until correspondence between you and the enquirer is at the point where the booking is contractual.

The number of

Confirmed

You have confirmed the booking - this means that you have agreed with the enquirer the details of the hire - dates, times, payments and costs and the agreement is contractual.
Completed

The booking date has passed. You can now mark it as completed.

Note: If you have a booking that is repeating, leave it as Confirmed until the final date of the booking has passed.

 

Replying to enquiries

  1. Open Lettings | Enquiries.
  2. Click the enquiry card to display the details of the enquiry message.
  3. Enter the reply into the Send a message box and click Reply.

    4. An email is sent to the enquirer with your reply. Any reply from the enquirer will be received in the Enquiries tab so you can follow all the correspondence there.

Confirming bookings

  1. Open Lettings | Enquiries.
  2. Click the Pending enquiry card to display the details of the enquiry.
  3. Click Confirm.
  4. In the Confirm Enquiry screen, enter the Scheduled date for the booking and any Additional information you need to give the enquirer, such as types of payment accepted, when payment should be received, where to check in, etc.

    5. Note: If this is a repeating booking, enter the Scheduled date for the first booking.

  5. Click Confirm.

    6. The confirmation is sent to the enquirer and you now have a confirmed booking.

Completing bookings

  1. Open Lettings | Enquiries.
  2. Click the Confirmed enquiry card to display the details of the booking.
  3. Click Complete.

    4. The booking is marked as completed.
