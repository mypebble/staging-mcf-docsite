Gift Aid made simple

Gift Aid is a tax concession where the UK government will not collect 25% in tax on donations where the donor is a UK taxpayer and the recipient has charitable status.

It may seem complex but Gift Aid is straightforward.

Are you eligible to claim Gift Aid?

The HMRC has guidance on the www.gov.uk website on all the rules around claiming Gift Aid. In summary, to claim Gift Aid:

  • You must be recognised as a charity or community amateur sports club (CASC) for tax purposes.

    • Academies get a charity number assigned when they set themselves up as an academy. Maintained schoolsA school in England or Wales that is funded by a local education authority. cannot become charities but may be able to acquire a form of charitable status through an associated Friends of trust. For more information, click here.

What do you need to do to claim Gift Aid?

Collect Gift Aid declarations

It is a legal requirement that you collect declaration documents that record who is giving you donations which are eligible for Gift Aid.

What are the rules?

  • You can only collect Gift Aid on donations made by UK taxpayers.
  • If you collect a Gift Aid declaration from a donor and make a Gift Aid claim, the donor is responsible for the statement they are a UK taxpayer within the given tax year of making the donation. If the statement isn’t true, HMRC will ask the donor to pay the equivalent amount.
    • It is important to remember that Gift Aid only equates to 25% of the amount donated. A donor would only be liable to pay the Gift Aid amount if they paid less tax in the current year than the amount of Gift Aid claimed on all their donations. For example, if a donor made five donations to the same charity in the between 5 April 2017 and 6 April 2018 at £12 each time and claimed eligibility for Gift Aid, you would receive £15 each time (£12 from the donor and £3 from HMRC) or £75 (£60 from the donor and £15 from HMRC). If the donor pays less than £15 tax in the tax year 5 April 2017 to 6 April 2018, he or she would be eligible to pay only the £15 given as Gift Aid instead of HMRC providing it.
  • If you collect Gift Aid without collecting a declaration from the donor, you are responsible and HMRC may ask you to repay the equivalent amount.

 

How can you collect Gift Aid declarations?

You can collect Gift Aid declarations from individual donors the following ways:

  • Issuing hard-copy declarations to donors to fill in. This happens when you download a PDF of the template from Campaigns > Stakeholders > Stakeholder Details.

  • Sending the online declaration form to donors to fill in. This happens when you (how? TBC).

  • Asking them to confirm at the time of online donation that they are eligible to give Gift Aid on top of the donation. For example, this is what happens when someone donates to a project by clicking on the Donate button on the project Community Page.

We have made it easy for you to collect multiple Gift Aid declarations at the same time by using a campaign:

  • Collect Gift Aid Declarations campaign. For more information, see Campaigns.

Note: You will be prompted to run this campaign as soon as you have three donations without Gift Aid attached.

Collecting back-dated Gift Aid declarations

If you are eligible to claim Gift Aid and you haven’t been doing so, you can start at any time and back-date claims for eligible donations made up to four years previously.

Tax years in the UK run from 6 April in one year to 5 April in the next year.

So if you are now in May 2017, you can collect Gift Aid on donations made since 6 April 2014 (6 April 2014 - 5 April 2015, 6 April 2015 - 5 April 2016, 6 April 2016 - 5 April 2017, and any donations since 6 April 2017.

All you need is to obtain a completed Gift Aid declaration from each donor who has made donations in the last four years.

Note:

You need to ensure that you have the correct registration for the donations made up to and since April 2016.

In April 2016, text was changed in Gift Aid declarations to be more robust and include the wording:

I am a UK taxpayer and understand that if I pay less Income Tax and/or Capital Gains Tax than the amount of Gift Aid claimed on all my donations in that tax year it is my responsibility to pay the difference.

If you obtained a Gift Aid declaration from a donor before April 2016, this declaration is valid for the donations made by the donor up to April 2016. You should obtain a new declaration for any donations made by the donor after April 2016.

 

You can use the Collect Gift Aid Declarations campaign to do this. For more information, see Campaigns.

Store Gift Aid declarations

You must keep Gift Aid declarations for six years from the end of the accounting period they relate to. This is a legal requirement. If you have a declaration that covers a repeating donation, you must keep these always. if the donor stops donating, then you must keep their declaration for six years following the end of the accounting period in which they made their last donation.

You can store hard-copy or online declarations. You must ensure that you are able to access all Gift Aid declarations for the accounting period in which a donation is made, and download the details when required for auditing purposes.
