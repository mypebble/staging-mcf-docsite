Sponsorship

The Importance of Sponsorship

Sponsors can be a mainstay of project support. Partnering with businesses is a mutually beneficial endeavour â€“ your school receives support for your projects (monetary or otherwise) and your business partner will reap rewards as well, such as fostering positive relations with their future workforce, promotional opportunities to members of your school community and fulfilling their corporate social responsibilities.

 

The Sponsorship module is where you can manage your relationships with your sponsors and prospective major donors. The layout of the module will ensure you are always up-to-date and know who to call upon when you need to ask for support for your projects. You can search across your sponsors for those who support different types of project and keep in touch with your sponsors to let them know how their sponsorship is making a difference to your project.

Four things to keep in mind when working with sponsors are:

  • Your project Joinos page will be a source of potential sponsorship leads.
  • Businesses and business owners will be interested in sponsoring school projects as part of their corporate social responsibility plans.
  • Face-to-face networking is also a mainstay of obtaining sponsors. Every social interaction is an opportunity to spread the word that you are looking for sponsors.

Managing sponsorship

When you access the Sponsorship module, you get an overview of your leads in a pipeline dashboard. This lets you see at a glance how your sponsorship is building - how many new leads you have to work with, how many have been qualified, pledged and completed. The goal is to get as many completed as possible, so it is very encouraging to see lots of new leads coming into the pipeline to work with and rewarding to see them move up the pipeline too.

The dashboard shows:

New Leads

 

This column shows the number of leads which have been added through your Joinos Community pages or added manually to the Sponsorship module.

If leads are from contacts and organisations that are not already in your Contacts module, these details are automatically added to the Contacts module.

You can assign a project to a new lead.

Qualified Leads

 

Once leads have been qualified manually (been approved by the Arro project owner and have an amount to pledge to the project) they show up in the Qualified Leads column.

You can record any received donation amounts and confirm the amount pledged by the sponsor.

Pledged

 

Once pledged leads have been confirmed and agreements have been signed or agreed to offline, the lead becomes a confirmed pledge and donations can be added here until the amount pledged is accrued.

Note: As well as adding donations, you can also subtract them here by entering a negative value.

Completed

 

Once the donations reach the pledged amount, the detail moves to the Completed column. However, you can continue to add donations should the sponsor want to donate more than the amount originally pledged.

Note: Clicking a card displays the card detail including the actions you need to move them along the pipeline. For example, leads move from New Leads to Qualified Leads when you click Confirm on the Qualify lead window, and leads move from Qualified Leads to Pledged when you click Confirm on the Confirm pledge window, and so on.

How do I build a sponsorship network?

When you first begin to use this module it will be empty and you need to build your sponsor network. Sponsors are added automatically when the sponsor enters their details on the Enquire Online form within the Sponsors section of your Joinos Community Pages. You can build your network by creating an email campaign within the Campaigns module to contact your existing contacts to invite them to sponsor your projects. For more information, see Email Campaigns. Within the Sponsorship module, you can add new leads manually.

Adding new leads

Leads are added automatically when the sponsor enters their details on the Sponsorship form on your Joinos Community Pages. You can add sponsorship records manually into the Sponsorship module.

Getting sponsors through your community page

  • Ensure your project Joinos page is published as the sponsorship form is available for selection on this page.

    • The sponsorship form collects the Name, Company, Telephone and Email address of the potential sponsor and automatically creates a sponsorship record in the Sponsorship module.

    When a sponsor is added automatically, you receive an email notification of the sponsorâ€™s interest so that you can follow up with them, and the sponsor receives on-screen confirmation that their enquiry has been sent. You will find the sponsor name listed in the New Leads section on the Sponsorship dashboard.

Manually from within the Sponsorship module

  1. Open Supporters > Sponsorship and click + Add direct enquiry.
  2. Enter the Name, Company and Email Address.
  3. Click Create.

Importing contacts from the Contacts module

  1. Open Supporters > Sponsorship and click + Add direct enquiry.
  2. Click Import contact.
  3. Click Import alongside the name of the contact in the list.

Viewing and adding notes

  1. Click the sponsor card.
  2. Enter the note into the Enter new note box.
  3. Click Add note.

Qualifying leads

Note: Paperwork and agreements for pledges are handled offline. You can qualify leads when you have contacted and discussed with the sponsor the project(s) they are interested in sponsoring and the amount they are able to pledge. This stage also includes you sending them details of the projects they are interested in.

  1. Open Sponsorship.
  2. Click the lead card in the New Leads column.
  3. Ensure that you select the project the lead is going to sponsor from the Project drop-down list so that the sponsorship is pledged correctly.
  4. Select Sent Details.
  5. Select Contacted.
  6. Select the amount the lead is going to pledge from the To Pledge drop-down list.
  7. Click Confirm.

Confirming pledges

Note: Paperwork and agreements for pledges are handled offline. Provided you have the agreed paperwork to support the confirmation, you can confirm pledges.

  1. Open Sponsorship.
  2. Click the card in the Qualified Leads column.
  3. Check the Sent Details and Contacted box.
  4. If an initial donation has been given, enter the received donation amount.
  5. Enter the confirmed amount that is pledged.
  6. Click Confirm.

Adding donations

At the Pledged stage, you can add donations as and when they come in from the sponsor. Only when the total agreed amount is entered does the card move to the Completed stage.

  1. Open Sponsorship.
  2. Click the card in the Pledged column.

    3. Note: On the card, the amount that has been pledged and the amount that has been donated to date are displayed along with the percentage complete value so you can see at a glance how the sponsorship pledge is doing.

  3. Enter the received donation amount.

    4. Note: If you need to reduce the donation amount, you can do this by entering a negative donation amount.

  4. Click Confirm

Archiving sponsors

Leads that donâ€™t work out or sponsors you want to remove are archived so you can see the history.

Archiving a lead

  1. Open Sponsorship.
  2. Click the lead card to archive.
  3. Click Archive.

Viewing archived leads

  • Open Sponsorship > Archived sponsors.

    • A list of archived sponsors is displayed.

Restoring archived leads

  1. Open Sponsorship > Archived sponsors.
  2. Select the check box alongside the sponsor names you want to restore.
  3. Click Restore selected.
