Selling school services in the wider community
You will find that you can sell your in-house expertise in the community during out of school hours. For example, if you have IT knowledge, or sport coaches within your knowledge bank at school, you can generate revenue by providing specialised assistance to organisations outside the school. This has two effects - it brings in more revenue to the school and it increases the community awareness about the school and it’s values and what it can offer the community.
In addition you can also trade your services with other schools, filling in the gaps for each other and generating revenue for all of you.

The Services module is where you can manage the portfolio of your school’s potentially profitable services and also manage the qualification / approval of individuals and organisation who request the use of your services.

In the Services module, you can:

  • Record and audit the services you offer.
  • Market your portfolio.

You can build your Services portfolio by adding items from scratch or by adding them from a number of templates (e.g. IT security, sports coaching, photocopying) and tailoring these to match your own offering. Once you have created items in your portfolio you can add images and conditions documents, so all of the information about what is on offer is held in the same place.

How do I build a Services portfolio?

When you first begin to use this module the Services dashboard will be empty and you are prompted to build your portfolio. You can add items from scratch or select add them by selecting from a library of templates and amend the details to match what your school offers. You can create services to share between your school and other schools or services to market to businesses and the wider community.

First time use
  1. Click Services.

    2. The Get Started screen is displayed.

  2. To watch an introductory video about services and lettings, click the Play button .
  3. To begin creating your portfolio of services, click Get Started. The Select Your Services page is displayed. This shows a library of templates, for generic services.
  4. Click Select on all of the services you have and would like to offer.
  5. Click Continue.

    6. Details for the first service you selected are displayed for you to amend and complete. The other services you selected are added to the Services dashboard as Incomplete so you can edit these in turn after the first.

    Note: If you want to change the services you chose, you can click Back and can then click Back to selection to return to the selection screen and choose different services.

  6. Edit the service details.

    7. Services which you want to share between schools should select the Private check box. This means the service is only available to those who you give the link to. Services which you want to make available to the public should leave the Private check box clear.

    Note: To make a service ready to publish, you must add a contact, a contact phone number and at least one image.

    1. Amend the details so these describe your service.
    2. To make a service private, click Private. If you want the service to be available to the public and wider community, leave the check box clear.
    3. Select a user from the Contact drop-down list and enter a Contact Phone Number.
    4. Add images for your service.
      1. Click + Add Image to add an image.
      2. Click Choose a file.
      3. Select the image to upload and click Open.
      4. Click Upload.

        5. Tip: If you upload an image you didn’t mean to, select the image. click Delete and confirm the deletion when prompted.

      5. If you upload more than one image, select which image is the primary image.

        6. Note: If you upload only one image this is the primary image by default.

  7. To save the details:
    • To save the details and publish later, click Save and publish later.
    • To publish now, click Publish now.

You can create services either from a template or from scratch, by clicking +New Service.

Once you have published services, the link to the published page is displayed at the top of the screen, along with Facebook and Twitter buttons for you to share details on social media. You can select the services you want to feature most prominently on your published Services page. If you don’t select any, we will select these automatically for you to ensure your page looks as good as it can.

Create a service

  1. Open Services.
  2. Click +New Service.

    3. To create a new service, you can:

    • Base the new service on an existing template from the library.
    • Create the service using a blank template.

Base a new service on an existing template

  1. Open Services.
  2. Click + New Service.
  3. Click Select to choose a template.
  4. Amend the template details as required.
  5. To make a service private, click Private. When published, it will only be available to those with whom you share the link.

    6. To make a service available to the public and wider community, leave the check box clear. When published it will appear on your services page.

  6. Add images for your service.
    1. Click + Add Image to add an image.
    2. Click Choose a file.
    3. Select the image to upload and click Open.
    4. Click Upload.

      5. Tip: If you upload an image you didn’t mean to, select the image. click Delete and confirm the deletion when prompted.

    5. If you upload more than one image, select which image is the primary image.

      6. Note: If you upload only one image this is the primary image by default.

  7. To save the details:
    • To save the details and publish later, click Save and publish later.
    • To publish now, click Publish now.

      • Note: To make a service ready to publish, you must add a contact, a contact phone number and at least one image.

Create a service, using a blank template

  1. Open Services.
  2. Click +New Service
  3. Click Select on Custom Service to display the Edit Your Service screen.
  4. Enter the Service Name.

    5. Tip: Choose a title which is short and to the point but easily identifies the facility e.g. IT Services, Printing, Football coaching, First aid.

  5. Enter your Summary. This is a short description e.g. Printing black and white and colour, variety of sizes and single or double-sided. We also offer perfect binding.
  6. Select a Contact from the drop-down list of contacts.
  7. Enter the Contact Phone Number.
  8. Enter the Description. This is an expansion on the summary.
  9. Enter the Key Features. These are the key selling points e.g. the level of accreditation for football coaching, first aid etc.
  10. Enter the Details for the hire. This is an expansion on the Description and can include full details of the services offered with the hire and whether there are conditions for the service.
  11. Enter the Availability, for example, the dates and times the service is available.
  12. Enter the Prices, for example, the rates of hiring, whether a deposit is required, what cancellation costs there are.
  13. If you have testimonials to include, enter the text in the Testimonial box. You should also enter the name of who has given the testimonial in the Endorser box.
  14. If you have someone who has used the facility and is willing to give a recommendation of it, you can enter their name in the Endorser box.
  15. To make a service private, click Private. When published, it will only be available to those with whom you share the link.

    16. To make a service available to the public and wider community, leave the check box clear. When published it will appear on your services page.

  16. Add images for your service.
    1. Click + Add Image to add an image.
    2. Click Choose a file.
    3. Select the image to upload and click Open.
    4. Click Upload.

      5. Tip: If you upload an image you didn’t mean to, select the image. click Delete and confirm the deletion when prompted.

    5. If you upload more than one image, select which image is the primary image.

      6. Note: If you upload only one image this is the primary image by default.

  17. To save the details:
    • To save the details and publish later, click Save and publish later.
    • To publish now, click Publish now.

    5. To make a service available to the public and wider community, leave the check box clear. When published it will appear on your services page.

    5. If you upload more than one image, select which image is the primary image.

    • If you have not previously published the page, and don’t want to publish it yet, click Save and publish later.

Manage images for a service

  • Click Edit on the service.
  1. To add an image:
    1. Click +Add Image.
    2. Click Choose a file and browse to select the image file.
    3. Click Upload.
  2. To mark an image as the primary image:
    1. Select the image you want to be the primary image.
    2. Click Set As Primary.
  3. To remove an image:
    1. Select the image you want to delete.
    2. Click Delete.

You can preview the details, by clicking Preview on the service card on the Services dashboard. Because the preview offers a draft view of the published layout, the Book Online buttons are greyed out.

Viewing published Services pages

  • Click View Page on a service which has been published.

    • Note: You can view the published page whether or not a service was marked as Private. The difference is that if it is private it will not appear on the services page advertising all the services you offer, and if it is not private then it will.

Archiving services

  1. Open Services.
  2. Click Edit on an Incomplete, Ready to publish or Published service.
  3. Click Archive.

Restoring archived services

  1. Open Services.
  2. Click Restore on an Archived service.

    3. The service restores with its previous status. For example, if it was published before being archived, it is restored as published. If it was archived before it was published and was incomplete, it is restored as incomplete. If it was archived when it was ready to publish, it is restored as ready to publish.

How do I publish and promote my services portfolio?

You can choose the services you want to feature prominently on your published services for hire page, although you do not need to wait to finish creating your portfolio before you publish the services which you are ready to market. You can work on draft services and only publish the ones with complete information.

Once you have published a service, you can begin to promote it, by linking to your published services page from the school website or by sharing its URL through social media.

Select featured services

  1. Open Services.
  2. Click Select Featured Services.
  3. Click Select Services.
  4. Click the services that you want to select. The favourite icon turns fully yellow when selected.
  5. Click Done.

Publish a service

  1. Open Services.
  2. Click Edit on an Incomplete or Ready to publish service.
  3. Click Publish now.

    4. Note: To make a service ready to publish, you must add a contact, a contact phone number and at least one image.

    The details are published on your hire.joinos.com page and Published is displayed at the top of the service details.

Promote and share your school services

You need to promote and share your services in order to make them work for the school. We offer different ways for you to do this. Your school services page can be advertised on your school website. You can also share links to each service for hire where appropriate. For example, if you are promoting your school football coaching expertise, you can share the link to this service for hire directly rather than the link to the school services page.

Sharing your school’s services page

  1. Open Services and you will see the details of Your Services Site at the top right hand of the screen:

    2. Note: You will only see this link once you have published at least one service.

  2. To share your school’s services page URL:
    1. Click Visit Site.
    2. Highlight the URL in the bar at the top of the site.
    3. Right-click and select Copy from the menu that appears.

      4. You can now paste the URL into a separate document so you can include it in your school website, or within email campaigns.

  3. To share your school’s services page on Facebook, click Facebook, log in to Facebook and share a post that links to the services page.
  4. To share your school’s services page on Twitter, click Twitter.
    • Authorising Pebble to tweet for you?

      You are asked to authorise Pebble to post tweets on your behalf. You only need to do this once.

      • If you are already logged in to Twitter:
        • Click Authorize App.
      • If you are not already logged in to Twitter:
        • Enter your school Twitter Username or email.
        • Enter your school Twitter Password.
        • Click Sign In.

      Authorisation is recorded within your school Twitter account and you are directed back to Arro which is redisplayed.

    • Enter the text you want to tweet and click Tweet.

      • Pebble posts the text as a tweet on the school twitter feed for you and a success message is shown.
