Volunteers

The Gift of Time

You will find that some people like to donate their time in addition to donating money, or just are more comfortable with or only able to contribute to your project through volunteering. People who have a desire to help by giving their time are invaluable assets to your income generation journey. Not only can they be called upon to perform labour-intensive tasks for your project, they are likely to be passionate about your project and are likely to spread the word, achieving a personal outreach that far exceeds traditional methods.

 

The Volunteers module is where you can manage your relationships with your network of volunteers, so you always have people to call upon to help you communicate your project needs to the wider community.

In the Volunteers module, you can search across your volunteers for those who are willing to do help out and for how much time they have pledged. For example, if you want people to hand out flyers for a particular project or to assist in other ways with the project, the Volunteers module will show you who is available for you to call upon.

You can build your volunteer network the following ways:

  • By running campaigns.

    • The Volunteers module links directly to the Campaigns module to let you send out a campaign asking for volunteers.

  • Through your Joinos Community pages where people can register an interest in volunteering to help you.
  • Word-of-mouth requests via other volunteers.

How do I build a volunteer network?

When you first begin to use this module it will be empty and you need to build your volunteer network. You can build your network by creating a campaign to invite your existing contacts to volunteer to support your projects and by adding volunteers manually.

Important! If you are adding volunteers who are going to work with children or adults, you need to ensure that the volunteer has passed the relevant DBS (previously CRB) checks. You will need to check this information outside Arro software in the usual way.

Running a campaign

  1. Open Supporters > Volunteers.
  2. Click Create volunteer campaign to invite your contacts to volunteer to support your projects.
  3. Preview the campaign.

    4. Tip: You can edit the text by clicking Please help and then clicking Edit.

Managing your volunteers

When you have some volunteers signed up, and you access the Volunteers module, you get an overview of your Volunteers in a dashboard.

The dashboard shows:

Leads

 

This column shows the number of leads which have been added through your Community pages or added manually.

If new leads are not already in your Contacts module, these are added to the Contacts module.

Active

 

Once volunteers are accepted and approved, they appear in the Active list. This list shows the contact details of the volunteer, the project they have chosen to help with and time pledged in hours.

Adding new volunteers

Leads are added automatically when the volunteer enters their details on the Volunteers form on your Joinos Community Pages. You can add volunteer records in the Volunteers module manually - adding their details directly.

Note: There is space for notes about the volunteers you add. If the volunteer is required for specific purposes that involves working directly with children, you should ensure that the volunteer has passed DBS (previously CRB) checks and record this in the notes for the volunteer. You can also use the Notes section to record other qualifications the volunteer has, for example, current qualification in first aid training has qualified first aid training. For more information, see Adding Notes.

Getting volunteers through your community pages

  • Ensure your project Joinos Community pages are published as the volunteer form is available for selection on these pages.

    • The volunteer form collects the Name, Email address, Telephone number and a message, if one is included, from the volunteer and automatically creates a volunteer record in the Volunteers module.

    When a volunteer is added automatically, you receive an email notification of the volunteer’s interest and the volunteer receives on-screen confirmation that their submission has been sent. You will find the volunteer name listed in the Leads section on the Volunteers dashboard.

Manually from within the Volunteers module

  1. Open Supporters > Volunteers.
  2. Click Add New.
  3. Enter the First Name, Last Name and Email Address.
  4. Click Create.

Importing contacts from the Contacts module

  1. Open Supporters > Volunteers.
  2. Click Add new
  3. Click Import contact.
  4. Click Import alongside the name of the contact in the list.

Confirming Volunteers

  1. Open Volunteers.
  2. Click Confirm on the card in the Leads column.
  3. Select the project the volunteer is going to help with from the Project drop-down list.
  4. Select Contacted.
  5. Enter the estimated amount of time the volunteer has pledged in hours.
  6. Click Notes to add notes if required. For more information, see Adding notes next.
  7. Click Confirm.

Adding notes

You can add notes for a volunteer lead. This gives you the ability to record the history of the contact you have with the volunteer.

Note: Add in this section, any notes applicable, for example, if the volunteer is going to work with children, has the volunteer passed the DBS (previously CRB) checks, or if they are trained in first aid when was their qualification date and last refresher date.

  1. Open Volunteers.
  2. Click the card in the Leads column or the Name in the Active column.
  3. Click Notes.
  4. Enter the note in the Enter new note box.
  5. Click Add note.

Archiving volunteers

Leads that don’t work out or volunteers you want to remove are archived so you can see the history. If the archival is temporary you can restore the lead at a later time.

Archiving a lead

  1. Open Volunteers.
  2. Click the lead to archive.
  3. Click Archive.

Viewing archived leads

  • Open Volunteers > Archived volunteers.

    • A list of archived volunteers is displayed.

Restoring archived leads

  1. Open Volunteers > Archived volunteers.
  2. Select the check box alongside the volunteer names you want to restore.
  3. Click Restore selected.
