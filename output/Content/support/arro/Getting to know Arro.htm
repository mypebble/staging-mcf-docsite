Getting to know Arro

Arro software is easy and intuitive to use. So what does it look like and how do you get started using it. When you first log on to Arro the dashboard is displayed in your browser window.

 

The Dashboard appears when you first log on to Arro and is also accessible by clicking the Arro logo or the school name. This is where you can quickly and easily see at a glance how your projects are progressing. You can view the information for all projects or for individual projects.

Your community Campaigns Notifications

You can see the total amount donated, the number of unique donations, number of sponsors and number of volunteers.

  • To view the community details for a specific project, select the project name from the All Projects drop-down.
  • To view Total Donated, Donors, Sponsors or Volunteers, click the relevant tab to display the information.

There’s information on your latest email campaign and how well it is doing. You can see when the campaign started and the average openThe number of times a marketing campaign email has been opened. and click ratesIndicates the number of times the link to your community page has been clicked. This can be from a hyperlink in an email during a marketing campaign, for example. and (for email campaigns). You can click Boost to get ideas on how to boost the open and click rates. In order to boost the campaign, click Boost your campaign.

You can see how many Twitter Likes you have received from your social media campaigns and click Boost to get ideas on how to improve that.

To help you address outstanding tasks quickly and easily there are prompts which alert you and clicking on the suggested action takes you straight to where you need to be. For example, if someone has donated some money to the project, you can thank them directly from here by clicking Say Thanks.

Note: These notifications are different from those you see in the header.

The Arro interface is split into the following sections:

Header

The header identifies the school and the logged on user. The notifications bell to the left of the logged on user name alerts you to new notifications since you were last in Arro.

Breadcrumbs

Additionally, once you select a module and drill-down into the screens, a breadcrumbA way of navigating around a system. A trail of breadcrumbs can lead you back to where you came from easily and provide short-cuts to different parts of the system. trail appears across the top of the screen to help you keep track of where you are in Arro.

In some cases you can click onto a level and go there directly. For example, clicking on Services and Lettings in this breadcrumb trail displays the Services and Lettings dashboard.

Breadcrumbs offer another way of navigating around Arro

Notifications

Arro provides notifications of things which are of interest to you, for example, when new grants become available. The notifications window is accessed by clicking on the bell icon next to your login name at the top of the screen.

When a new notification becomes available, Arro alerts you to this by showing a number in red of the new notifications that have appeared. For example, if you receive one new alert for new grants, you would see Alerts of notifications. You receive notifications in Arro from the bell icon next to your logon name at the top of the screen.

To view the notifications in the Notifications window, click the notifications icon.

To go to the relevant module in Arro, click the notification, for example, to see new grants, click the notification New grants are available ...

Note: You may receive notifications at any time, so be alert to the number in red appearing and you can then check what these are. If you are not logged onto Arro, you will receive an email notification when there is a new alert within Arro.

Navigation bar

The Navigation Bar shows all the modules you have in ArroArro and you can select any from here. In addition to modules, the Navigation Bar has a Help & Support option which opens the Support website for Arro.

Work area

This is where you work within each module. The example shows the Projects module in the work area.

Arro makes use of many usual features such as tabbed menus, buttons to cause actions, data entry forms to record data, fields that are hyperlinked for drill-down, and so on.

Tabbed menus

The Arro dashboard doesn’t have tabbed menus, but the Projects module does.

These let you choose different views in the work area.

Action buttons

Buttons let you perform actions. For example, to create a new project, you would click + New Project on the Projects list.

Hyperlink drill-downs

Where text is hyperlinked, you can click the link to drill-down to more detail.

Data Entry forms

These let you enter the data you need, for example, enter contact details.

Data entry forms include different types of fields. The New Contact form contains:

Selection field

You select from a drop-down of available options, for example the Contact Type field.

Data Entry field

You enter free text in these, for example, the First Name field.

Elsewhere in Arro you may encounter the following types of field in data forms:

Check box

You click to select and click again to clear, for example, a project in the Projects list.

Date field

You select the date from the drop-down calendar, for example, the First Email field when sending a campaign.

Filter fields

A filter works to shorten a lengthy list of items. Filtering can be done several ways. In this example from the Grants module,

you can:

  • Enter free text in the Search field. This will shorten the list to show only those grants that include the free text which you have entered.
  • Click the first selection field in red School Type to select the school type and click the second selection field in red Location to select the location. This will shorten the list to show only those that are available for the school type and in the location you have chosen.
  • Select from a drop-down list of filters - Project Type, Grant Type, Complexity or Status. This will shorten the list to show only those which fulfil the criteria.
  • Click at the right-hand side of the Deadline column, to view grants you have previously saved as favourites.

    • For more information on saving grants, see Save grants as favourites.

To move between fields in data entry forms, either use the mouse to click in the relevant field or use the TAB key to move from field to field.

Scrolling in drop-downs - how to do it

You can scroll in drop-downs either using the scroll bar in the drop-down or using the mouse scroll wheel when the mouse cursor is placed on the drop-down list.
